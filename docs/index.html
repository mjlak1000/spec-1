01 // The problem

The bottleneck in intelligence work is not information. It is attention. Feeds from think tanks, investigative outlets, and defense research organizations produce dozens of pieces every day. Most are not actionable. A small fraction are. The challenge is triage.

Most tools do not solve this. They aggregate. They surface the same noise forever because nothing about their output feeds back into their input. The loop never closes.

SPEC-1 closes the loop. Every signal scored. Every outcome classified. Thresholds updated before the next cycle runs. The brief tomorrow is shaped by what was accurate today.

The four-gate scoring framework

Gate 1
Credibility
Source editorial standards, domain expertise, track record. Lower-rated sources do not advance regardless of content. Internal ratings — not published.
Gate 2
Volume
Sufficient content depth. Brief posts and summaries filtered out. Only signals with meaningful substance continue.
Gate 3
Velocity
Intelligence value degrades rapidly. Scoring rewards recency, penalizes stale signals on a sliding scale across time windows.
Gate 4
Novelty
High-value domain keyword match. Inconsequential topics excluded regardless of source credibility. Keyword taxonomy not published.

05 // The daily brief

After each cycle Claude Sonnet writes a structured intelligence brief. Every claim traces to a scored signal. Six sections — every morning, every cycle.

SectionWhat it contains
Executive Summary3 sentences. What happened. What it means. What is uncertain.
Elevated SignalsOne paragraph per elevated signal with confidence score and watch directive
Domain BriefingsGeopolitics narrative · Cyber/Info Ops narrative — patterns, not lists
Story Leads3–5 actionable leads: the question, who to call, documents to request, freshness window, confidence flag
Research PromptsCopy-paste Claude prompts shipped with each lead — ready to use immediately
Watch List3–5 specific things to monitor tomorrow, tied to today's signals

Investigation workspace

Added April 2026. Open a case on any topic — a name, a pattern, a hunch. Every morning SPEC-1 checks incoming signals against every open case. Matches go into the case file. Claude writes a finding update. The brief includes a case status section.

Cases stay open until you close them. Closing writes a final report. Persistent evidence accumulation across weeks or months.

06 // How it learns

No neural network. No gradient descent. The scoring framework is rule-based and deterministic. But the system improves — through explicit, auditable calibration rather than implicit weight adjustment.

A neural network learns implicitly. SPEC-1 learns explicitly. The tradeoff is transparency and control in exchange for automation. When a threshold is wrong, the failure is diagnosable. The specific threshold that failed can be identified and adjusted.

Source credibility ratings update as publishers change. The analyst registry grows as experts establish track records. The test suite — 519 tests — encodes the accumulated judgment of every calibration decision. Changes can be made and validated without silent regressions.

The 519 tests are not incidental. They are the changelog of every threshold decision made.
